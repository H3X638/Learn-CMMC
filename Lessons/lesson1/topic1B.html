Topic 1B: DFARS, FAR, and NIST SP 800-171

Learn how DFARS and FAR clauses establish cybersecurity expectations for defense contractors and how NIST SP 800-171 serves as the foundation for compliance.

15–30 minutes 15 knowledge points

The Purpose of Federal Cybersecurity Clauses

The U.S. government relies heavily on private contractors to provide goods and services that often involve sensitive but unclassified information. To protect this data, cybersecurity requirements are built directly into federal contracts through key regulatory clauses:

  • FAR 52.204-21: Establishes basic safeguarding requirements for all federal contractors.
  • DFARS 252.204-7012: Adds enhanced requirements for DoD contractors handling Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI).

FAR 52.204-21: Basic Safeguarding

The FAR clause applies broadly to any company doing business with the federal government. It defines 15 baseline security controls, such as limiting system access to authorized users, protecting connections to public networks, and sanitizing media before disposal. These practices set the minimum cybersecurity expectations for handling federal information.

DFARS 252.204-7012: Safeguarding CUI

DFARS 7012 applies when contractors handle CUI. It requires implementation of NIST SP 800-171 controls, reporting cyber incidents within 72 hours, and preserving forensic evidence. Prime contractors must also ensure their subcontractors meet the same requirements, extending CUI protection throughout the supply chain.

NIST SP 800-171: The Technical Blueprint

NIST SP 800-171 outlines 110 security requirements across 14 control families such as Access Control, Incident Response, and System Integrity. These requirements form the technical foundation of CMMC Level 2, defining how organizations safeguard CUI in practice.

Connecting DFARS, FAR, and CMMC

These frameworks interconnect:

  • FAR 52.204-21: Supports CMMC Level 1 (Foundational).
  • DFARS 252.204-7012: Aligns with CMMC Level 2 (Advanced).
  • NIST SP 800-171: Defines the technical implementation of DFARS 7012.

Together, they create a compliance staircase that begins with FAR’s basic safeguarding and culminates in CMMC’s third-party verification process.

Key Takeaways

  • FAR 52.204-21 sets basic cybersecurity requirements for all federal contractors.
  • DFARS 252.204-7012 extends protections to include Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI).
  • NIST SP 800-171 provides the technical framework for compliance.
  • Together, these clauses form the foundation for CMMC Level 1 and Level 2.
